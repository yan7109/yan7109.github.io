From "Reading the Inbound Email" — Who Sent It?

The identity and role of the sender tells you more than anything they write. Here is the hierarchy, ranked from most actionable to least:

  • 1
    CEO / Founder
    If a CEO is spending time on this, significant internal deliberation has already happened. This is the most serious signal you can receive.
  • 2
    Board Member / Investor
    The CEO delegated this to someone experienced in deals. Strong signal — executive-level interest exists.
  • 3
    Product Executive
    A senior product leader has identified your company as relevant to their roadmap. Likely strategic in nature.
  • 4
    Engineering Executive
    Almost always acquihire. They want your engineers, and possibly your technology.
  • 5
    PE / Financial Buyer
    Their motivations are fundamentally different — they're evaluating your company as a financial investment.
  • 6
    Banker
    The acquiring company hired a banker to identify targets. You're one of several being evaluated.
  • 7
    Corp Dev / Analyst
    The least actionable. This does not mean an executive has decided to acquire you.

The full guide includes detailed analysis of each tier, what to expect, and how to calibrate your response.

From "Reading the Inbound Email" — Annotated Example

Hi [Founder],

[Investor Name] mentioned that you and I should connect. I've been personally following [Your Company]'s growth and I think there's a compelling strategic rationale for our companies to do something together. I'd love to find time for a call this week if possible.

[CEO Name]

Reading this email:
  • Sender: CEO. The most senior person at the company. Highest actionability.
  • Via investor: Trust transfer from a credible source. The investor vetted this.
  • "Personally following" — This has been on their radar. Not a cold impulse.
  • "Compelling strategic rationale" — They've thought about the "why." Not generic curiosity.
  • "This week" — Urgency. They want to move.

Verdict: This is the most serious inbound you'll receive. The full guide includes 4 annotated examples across different acquirer types, plus reply templates for each.
